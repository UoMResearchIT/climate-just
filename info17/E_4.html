Physical Geography

Indicator

Distance to coast

Assumption

The closer an area is to the coast, the more likely it is to be cooler relative to areas inland.

Confidence level

Medium

Guidance for the use of this indicator

This indicator accounts for the additional impact that physical location can have during events like heat-waves. All other things being equal, coastal areas are more likely to be cooler compared to inland areas due to the effects of onshore breezes. Additional, more refined, data may be available at the local level, e.g. through data agreements with the Ordnance Survey.
