Item Description
Reference E_6
Theme Vulnerability
Hazard reference Heat
Dimension Enhanced Exposure
Domain Physical Geography
Indicator Elevation
Assumption The more elevated an area, the more likely it is to be cooler relative to more low lying areas.
Confidence level Medium
Guidance for the use of this indicator This indicator accounts for the additional impact that physical location can have during events like heat-waves. All other things being equal, areas at higher elevations are more likely to be cooler compared to lower lying areas. Additional, more refined, data may be available at the local level, e.g. through data agreements with the Ordnance Survey.
Data Source Generated from US Geological Survey (unrestricted) Digital Elevation Model GTOPO30 (equivalent to 1km spatial resolution), see https://lta.cr.usgs.gov/GTOPO30 for more information. Elevation at 2011 population weighted centroids.
